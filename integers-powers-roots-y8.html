1.1a · Negative Integers

All Operations with Negative Numbers

Foundation
Sign rules for × and ÷:
same signs → positive  |  different signs → negative
Adding negatives = subtracting. Subtracting a negative = adding.

Worked Example

Calculate:  −6 × 4 + 18 ÷ (−3)

Use BIDMAS: Division and Multiplication first.   −6 × 4 = −24   (different signs → −)
18 ÷ (−3) = −6   (different signs → −)
Now add:   −24 + (−6) = −24 − 6 = −30

✏ Your Turn

Calculate:  −5 × (−3) + 20 ÷ (−4)
Evaluate:  −8 + 3 × (−2) − (−10)
What is  −42  and why is it different from  (−4)2?
1.  −5 × (−3) = +15;  20 ÷ (−4) = −5;  15 + (−5) = 10
2.  3 × (−2) = −6;  −8 + (−6) − (−10) = −8 − 6 + 10 = −4
3.  −4² = −(4²) = −16;  (−4)² = +16. The brackets change which operation happens first.

1.1c · Primes, HCF & LCM

Prime Factor Decomposition, HCF and LCM

Foundation

Worked Example: Write 360 as a product of prime factors

Divide repeatedly by the smallest prime that goes in: 360 ÷ 2 = 180
180 ÷ 2 = 90  →  90 ÷ 2 = 45  →  45 ÷ 3 = 15  →  15 ÷ 3 = 5  →  5 ÷ 5 = 1
Collect: 360 = 23 × 32 × 5

Worked Example: HCF and LCM of 24 and 36
