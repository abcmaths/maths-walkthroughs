3.2b · Construction

Geometric Constructions

Foundation
Always use a ruler and compasses for constructions — do not erase your arcs. Examiners look for construction marks as evidence of method.

Perpendicular Bisector of AB

Open compasses to more than half of AB. Place point on A, draw arcs above and below the line.
Without changing the radius, place the compass point on B and draw two more arcs crossing the first pair.
Draw a straight line through the two intersection points. This line is the perpendicular bisector of AB — it passes through the midpoint of AB at 90°.

Angle Bisector

Place compass point at the vertex. Draw an arc that crosses both arms of the angle.
From each crossing point, draw equal arcs inside the angle.
Draw a straight line from the vertex through the intersection of the two arcs.
