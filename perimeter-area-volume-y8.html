3.3a · Area of Trapezium

Area of a Trapezium

Foundation
Area = ½ × (a + b) × h   where a and b are the parallel sides and h is the perpendicular height.
Identify the parallel sides: a = 8 cm, b = 13 cm
Identify the perpendicular height: h = 6 cm
Apply the formula: Area = ½ × (8 + 13) × 6 = ½ × 21 × 6 = 63 cm²

E A trapezium has area 84 cm², height 7 cm, and one parallel side of 9 cm. Find the other parallel side.
1. ½ × (5+11) × 4 = 32 cm²
2. ½ × (7+15) × 9 = 99 m²
3. 84 = ½ × (9+b) × 7 → 24 = 9+b → b = 15 cm
3.3b · Volume of Prisms

Volume of a Prism

Foundation
Volume of any prism = Area of cross-section × length
For a cuboid: V = l × w × h

Worked Example: Volume of cuboid 12 × 5 × 3

V = l × w × h = 12 × 5 × 3
= 180 cm³

Worked Example: Triangular prism

Cross-section is a right triangle with base 6 cm, height 4 cm. Prism length = 10 cm.

Area of triangle = ½ × 6 × 4 = 12 cm²
Volume = 12 × 10 = 120 cm³

✏ Your Turn

E A trapezoidal prism has parallel sides of 6 cm and 10 cm, height 4 cm, and length 15 cm. Find the volume.
1. 8 × 4 × 2.5 = 80 cm³
2. Area = ½×5×8 = 20 cm² → V = 20×12 = 240 cm³
3. Area = ½×(6+10)×4 = 32 cm² → V = 32×15 = 480 cm³
3.3c · Compound Shapes

Perimeter and Area of Compound Shapes

Foundation

Strategy

Split the shape into simple shapes (rectangles, triangles, etc.).
Find the area of each part, then add (or subtract if a section is removed).
For perimeter, only count the outer boundary — do not include internal dividing lines.

✏ Your Turn

An L-shaped room has outer dimensions 10 m × 8 m. A 4 m × 3 m rectangle is cut from one corner.

Find the area of the L-shape.
Find the perimeter of the L-shape.
E A compound shape is a rectangle 12 × 5 with a semicircle of diameter 5 added to one short end. Find the total area (π = 3.142).
1. Large rectangle = 80 m²; cut = 12 m² → Area = 68 m²
2. Outer boundary: 10 + 8 + 4 + 3 + 6 + 5 = 36 m (depends on exact shape — check with a sketch)
3. Rectangle = 60 cm²; semicircle = ½π(2.5)² ≈ 9.82 cm² → Total ≈ 69.8 cm²
