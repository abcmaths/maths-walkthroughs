3.1a · Parallel Lines

Alternate, Corresponding & Co-interior Angles

Foundation
Co-interior angles (also called allied or C-angles) are between the parallel lines on the same side of the transversal. They add up to 180°.

✏ Your Turn

Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. One alternate angle is 65°. Find the other alternate angle and give a reason.
A corresponding angle pair has one angle of 112°. Find the other and state why.
Co-interior angles are (3x + 10)° and (2x + 20)°. Find x and both angles.
1. 65° — alternate angles are equal (Z angles).
2. 112° — corresponding angles are equal (F angles).
3. Sum = 180°: 5x + 30 = 180 → x = 30 → angles are 100° and 80°
3.1b · Polygon Angles

Interior & Exterior Angles of Polygons

FoundationExtended
Sum of interior angles of an n-sided polygon = (n − 2) × 180°
Each exterior angle of a regular n-gon = 360° ÷ n
Interior + exterior = 180° (on a straight line)

Worked Example: Sum of interior angles of a 9-sided shape

(n − 2) × 180° = (9 − 2) × 180°
= 7 × 180° = 1260°

✏ Your Turn

Find the sum of interior angles of a 12-sided polygon.

A regular hexagon has one interior angle labelled x. Find x.
E The interior angles of a pentagon are x, 2x, 90°, 110°, and 130°. Find x.
1. (12−2)×180 = 1800°
2. 360 ÷ 24 = 15 sides
3. 720 ÷ 6 = 120°
4. x + 2x + 90 + 110 + 130 = 540 → 3x = 210 → x = 70°
