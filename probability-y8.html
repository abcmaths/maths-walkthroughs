4.2a · Simple Probability

Probability Scale & Basic Probability

Foundation
P(event) = number of favourable outcomes ÷ total equally likely outcomes
Probability is always between 0 (impossible) and 1 (certain).

Worked Example: Bag with 5 red, 3 blue, 2 green marbles

Total marbles = 5 + 3 + 2 = 10
P(blue) = 3/10 = 0.3
P(not green) = 1 − 2/10 = 8/10 = 4/5

4.2b · Experimental vs Theoretical

Experimental vs Theoretical Probability

FoundationExtended
Theoretical probability: calculated from equally likely outcomes (e.g. fair die → P(4) = 1/6).
Experimental (relative frequency): frequency of outcome ÷ total trials.
As the number of trials increases, experimental probability gets closer to theoretical.

Worked Example: Biased coin, 30 flips

OutcomeFrequencyRelative FrequencyTheoretical (fair)
Heads1818/30 = 0.600.50
Tails1212/30 = 0.400.50
Total301.001.00
The relative frequency for heads (0.60) differs from the theoretical (0.50) → the coin may be biased towards heads.
With only 30 trials there is significant random variation. More trials would give a more reliable estimate.
