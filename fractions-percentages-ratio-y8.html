1.3a · Fraction Arithmetic

Adding, Subtracting, Multiplying and Dividing Fractions

Foundation

Worked Example: ¾ + ⅔

Find the LCM of the denominators: LCM(4, 3) = 12
Convert: ¾ = 9/12   and   ⅔ = 8/12
Add: 9/12 + 8/12 = 17/12 = 1 5/12
Multiply fractions: multiply top × top and bottom × bottom, then simplify.
Divide fractions: flip the second fraction and multiply (KCF: Keep, Change, Flip).

E A recipe needs 2¼ cups of flour. How many full recipes can be made from a 9-cup bag?
1. LCM(6,4)=12: 10/12 − 3/12 = 7/12
2. 5/2 × 4/3 = 20/6 = 10/3 = 3⅓
3. 3/5 × 7/4 = 21/20 = 1 1/20
4. 9 ÷ 2¼ = 9 ÷ 9/4 = 9 × 4/9 = 4 → 4 full recipes

Percentage Calculations

Foundation
TaskMethodExample

% increaseamount × multiplier (>1)Increase £85 by 12%: £85 × 1.12 = £95.20
% decreaseamount × multiplier (<1)Decrease 200 by 15%: 200 × 0.85 = 170
% change(change ÷ original) × 10050 → 65: (15 ÷ 50) × 100 = 30%

Worked Example: % Increase from 50 to 65

Find the change: 65 − 50 = 15
Divide by original: 15 ÷ 50 = 0.3
Multiply by 100: 0.3 × 100 = 30%

1.3c · Ratio

Ratio Sharing

Foundation

Worked Example: Divide 120 in the ratio 2 : 3 : 5

Add the parts: 2 + 3 + 5 = 10 parts
Find one part: 120 ÷ 10 = 12
Multiply: 2×12 = 24, 3×12 = 36, 5×12 = 60
Check: 24 + 36 + 60 = 120 ✓
