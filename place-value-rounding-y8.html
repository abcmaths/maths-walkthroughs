1.2a · Significant Figures

Rounding to Significant Figures

Foundation
Key rule: Count s.f. from the first non-zero digit. Round the next digit: if ≥ 5, round up; if < 5, round down. Fill remaining places with zeros as placeholders.

Worked Example: Round 0.006743 to 2 s.f.

Ignore leading zeros — they are not significant. The first s.f. is 6.
The second s.f. is 7. Look at the next digit: 4.
Since 4 < 5, round down (leave 7 unchanged). Answer: 0.0067

1.2b · Ordering

Ordering Integers and Decimals

Foundation

Worked Example: Order −2.3, 0.5, −1.8, 2.1

Answer (smallest first): −2.3 < −1.8 < 0.5 < 2.1
Tip: on a number line, further left = smaller. Negative numbers with a larger absolute value are smaller.

1.2c · Standard Form

Standard Form (Powers of 10)

Extended
Standard form: A × 10n where 1 ≤ A < 10 and n is an integer.
Large numbers: n is positive. Small numbers: n is negative.

Worked Example: Write 4 500 000 in standard form

Write the digits with a decimal point after the first digit: 4.5
Count how many places the decimal point moves left to reach the original number: 6 places → 106
Answer: 4.5 × 106

Worked Example: Multiply 3.6 by 0.01

0.01 = 10−2. Multiplying by 10−2 moves the decimal 2 places left.
3.6 → 0.036.   Answer: 0.036
