Converting Between Units

Foundation

3.5b · Speed, Distance, Time

Speed = Distance ÷ Time

Foundation
The SDT triangle: Cover the quantity you want — the remaining two show the operation.
Speed = D ÷ T  |  Distance = S × T  |  Time = D ÷ S

Worked Example

A car travels 240 km in 3 hours. Find its average speed.

Identify: D = 240 km, T = 3 h, S = ?
S = D ÷ T = 240 ÷ 3 = 80 km/h

Worked Example — time conversion

A cyclist travels at 15 km/h for 40 minutes. How far do they travel?

Convert time to hours: 40 min = 40/60 = 2/3 h
D = S × T = 15 × 2/3 = 10 km

✏ Your Turn

Use the triangle to help.

A bus travels 180 km at 60 km/h. How long does the journey take? Give your answer in hours and minutes.
A runner completes a race at 8 m/s in 45 seconds. Find the distance of the race.
E A train leaves station A at 09:15 and arrives at station B (240 km away) at 11:45. Find the average speed.
1. T = 180 ÷ 60 = 3 h = 3 hours 0 minutes
2. D = 8 × 45 = 360 m
3. Time = 11:45 − 09:15 = 2.5 h; Speed = 240 ÷ 2.5 = 96 km/h
3.5c · Scale Drawings

Reading and Interpreting Scale Drawings

Foundation
Scale is written as a ratio, e.g. 1 : 50 000 means 1 cm on the map = 50 000 cm (= 500 m = 0.5 km) in reality.
Actual distance = map distance × scale factor  |  Map distance = actual distance ÷ scale factor

Worked Example

A map has scale 1 : 25 000. Two towns are 8 cm apart on the map. Find the real distance.

Real distance = 8 × 25 000 = 200 000 cm
Convert: 200 000 cm ÷ 100 000 = 2 km

✏ Your Turn

A scale drawing uses the scale 1 : 200. A room measures 3.5 cm on the drawing. Find the real length in metres.
A field is 350 m long. On a map with scale 1 : 5000, how long is it in cm?
E A map has scale 1 : 50 000. Two cities are 14.6 km apart. How far apart are they on the map in cm?
1. 3.5 × 200 = 700 cm = 7 m
2. 350 m = 35 000 cm; 35 000 ÷ 5000 = 7 cm
3. 14.6 km = 1 460 000 cm; 1 460 000 ÷ 50 000 = 29.2 cm
